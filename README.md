\# Telecom Customer Churn Analysis Dashboard



\## Project Overview



This project analyzes customer churn for a fictional telecom company in California. The goal is to identify which customer segments are most likely to churn, understand the main churn drivers, and estimate the financial impact of customer loss.



The dashboard was built in Tableau using the Maven Analytics Telecom Customer Churn dataset.



\## Business Objective



The main business question is:



\*\*Why are customers leaving, which segments are most at risk, and how much revenue is being lost?\*\*



This dashboard helps business stakeholders identify high-risk customer groups and prioritize retention strategies.



\## Dataset



The dataset includes customer-level telecom data such as:



\* Customer status

\* Contract type

\* Tenure in months

\* Monthly charges

\* Total revenue

\* Churn category

\* Churn reason

\* Internet service type

\* Payment method

\* City and zip code



The dataset contains 7,043 customer records and includes California location data.



\## Key Metrics



The dashboard tracks the following KPIs:



\* Total Customers: 7,043

\* Churned Customers: 1,869

\* Churn Rate: 28.4%

\* Monthly Revenue Lost: $137,087

\* Average Tenure of Churned Customers: 18.0 months



\## Dashboard Features



The dashboard includes:



\* KPI cards for customer and revenue metrics

\* Churn rate by contract type

\* Churn rate by tenure group

\* Revenue lost by churn category

\* Top churn reasons

\* Top cities by churned customers



\## Key Insights



\* Month-to-month customers have the highest churn rate at 51.7%.

\* Customers in their first 6 months have the highest churn risk at 77.2%.

\* Competitor-related churn is the largest driver of monthly revenue loss.

\* The top five churn reasons account for nearly 60% of churned customers.

\* San Diego and Los Angeles have the highest churn volume among California cities.



\## Business Recommendations



Based on the analysis, the company should:



\* Improve onboarding for customers in their first 6 months.

\* Encourage month-to-month customers to move to longer-term contracts.

\* Create competitive retention offers for high-risk customers.

\* Improve customer support experience to reduce service-related churn.

\* Monitor high-volume churn cities for targeted retention campaigns.
